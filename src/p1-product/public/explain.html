The Problem
Interview prep is broken.
And nobody's fixed the real thing.
Every candidate reads articles, watches YouTube, memorises bullet points. Then they sit in the interview — and freeze. Because reading about something and being able to explain it under pressure are completely different skills. Nobody is training the skill that actually matters.
"If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough."
— Richard Feynman, Nobel Laureate in Physics
📚
Passive learning doesn't work
Flashcards, videos, and notes create the illusion of understanding. The interview exposes the truth — immediately.
🎭
Filler words destroy credibility
"Um", "basically", "sort of thing" — interviewers count these. Candidates don't even know they're doing it.
🎯
Generic prep misses the brief
A .NET/Angular/Azure SQL role needs completely different preparation to a .NET/React/Python role. Generic prep fails both.
📊
No measurable progress
Candidates have no idea if they're improving. There's no score, no feedback, no data — just hope.
The Product
Three layers. One complete system.
Explain is built in three layers that work independently and compound together. Each layer adds value on its own. Together they create something nobody else has.
01
The Candidate App
Speak. Get scored. Get better. Repeat.
A candidate picks a subject — "Tell me about microservices" or "Explain your biggest career mistake" — and speaks for 60–120 seconds. AI scores their explanation across five dimensions in real time. Their history tracks improvement across every session.
  • AI-generated lesson on any subject — understand before you explain
  • Timed talk session with transcription
  • Scored on Clarity, Confidence, Depth, Accuracy, Structure (0–100)
  • Full AI coaching feedback after every session
  • Session history with improvement trends over time
On content accuracy: AI-generated lessons carry hallucination risk — a wrong technical lesson in interview prep does real damage at the worst possible moment. Grounding lesson content against verified sources is a near-term engineering priority, particularly as we move into B2B where recruiter trust in our scores is the whole product.
B2C · Consumer App
02
Job Spec Upload
Upload the actual job description. Get prep built for that exact role.
The candidate or recruiter uploads the real job specification. AI reads the requirements — tech stack, seniority, domain — and generates targeted interview questions and subject material specific to that role. A £600k quant-developer role in New York gets completely different prep to a £80k React developer role in Manchester.
  • Upload PDF or paste job description
  • AI extracts role requirements, seniority, domain, tech stack
  • Generates 10–20 targeted practice questions
  • Creates subject lessons for each key area
  • Works across every industry and seniority level
New · Unique Feature
03
The Recruiter Portal
Recruiters send prep. Candidates arrive ready. Everyone wins.
A recruitment firm logs into their portal, uploads a job spec, and assigns a tailored interview prep package to a candidate. The candidate gets a notification, completes the sessions, and the recruiter sees their readiness scores before the interview. This is the B2B product that no recruitment software has ever thought to build.
  • Recruiter dashboard — manage candidates and live roles
  • Upload job spec → assign prep to candidate in 2 clicks
  • Candidate gets push notification with their prep package
  • Recruiter sees candidate's practice scores and filler word trends
  • Billed per candidate prep or monthly subscription per recruiter seat

Candidate Privacy — A Key Design Principle
Practice privately. Submit only when you're ready.
Nobody wants their recruiter seeing their first attempt at explaining microservices at 11pm. Candidates can practice as many times as they like — all sessions are private by default. Only when they're happy with a run do they tap Submit This Attempt — and only that score reaches the recruiter. Nothing else.

Not a practice tool.
A simulation you can't tell apart from the real thing.
The candidate faces a live panel of AI interviewers — each with a name, a role, and a personality. One evaluates. One deep-dives. One challenges everything. The candidate answers in real time, on camera, just like the actual interview. It's the closest preparation possible without sitting in the room — and it's interactive, scored, and repeatable as many times as they need.

The Differentiator
Filler Word Intelligence — woven into job-spec prep and the recruiter loop.
Interviewers notice filler words. Candidates don't know they're using them. After every session, Explain analyses the full transcript for filler words, filler phrases, pacing, pauses, repetition and vocabulary richness. Across multiple sessions, it tracks whether they're improving — or getting worse under pressure. This is the data that changes behaviour. Nobody forgets their score the first time they see "basically × 18."
On the competitive landscape: Yoodli (well-funded, US) does filler-word analysis and speech coaching well. Google's Interview Warmup exists too. Neither has a recruiter workflow, job-spec-tailored content, or a learning engine underneath. Our moat is the combination — nobody else closes the loop from job spec → tailored prep → candidate practice → recruiter readiness scores. That's what makes this a platform, not a feature.

AI Interview Coaching
Not just scored. Coached.
Like a personal interview trainer in your pocket.
Scoring tells you what happened. Coaching tells you what to do about it. After every session, Explain's AI coach delivers personalised, actionable feedback — the kind that used to cost hundreds of pounds an hour from a human career coach. And it knows the difference between a technical interview, a competency interview, and a senior leadership assessment.
🎯
Competency-Based Interview Coaching
The STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is the gold standard for civil service, NHS, graduate schemes and corporate roles. Explain coaches candidates on structuring answers, choosing the right stories from their experience, and delivering them fluently under pressure — not just knowing the framework, but owning it.
💬
The Personal Questions Most People Fail
"Tell me about yourself." "What's your biggest weakness?" "Where do you see yourself in five years?" These questions trip up even the most technically brilliant candidates. Explain coaches the personal narrative — how to tell your story authentically, how to frame weaknesses as growth, how to sell ambition without arrogance.
🧠
Technical Explanation Coaching
For technical roles, being right isn't enough — you have to explain it clearly to a non-technical panel. Explain coaches candidates on layering technical depth: the simple answer first, the detail on request, the analogy that lands with a business stakeholder. The skill that separates senior engineers from junior ones.
📈
Personalised to Your Weak Points
After each session, the AI identifies your specific patterns — too many fillers, answers that trail off, missing the Action in your STAR answers, vocabulary that's too informal. Coaching targets exactly what that individual needs to fix, not generic advice that applies to everyone and helps no one.
Why Local Government & Public Sector Love This
Competency-based interviewing is mandatory across the entire UK public sector. Every council, every NHS trust, every civil service department runs structured STAR interviews — and spends significant budget on coaching candidates through them. Explain delivers that coaching at scale, consistently, measurably, and at a fraction of the cost. This is exactly what employability teams, careers services and HR departments have been waiting for.
